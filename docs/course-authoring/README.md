# Course authoring

One workstream owns one subject, learner band, source set, content directory, fixtures, misconception tests and evidence notes. Authors separate source facts, generated pedagogy, private evaluator truth and learner-visible content.

Release requires span/hash checks, answer privacy, deterministic answer/rubric truth, runtime compatibility, one critic and at most one typed repair, fail-closed publication, accessibility notes and a transfer task without Maia/hints/solutions. External standards require provenance and license review; generated content is never silently official curriculum.

## Shipped authored paths

- [`Algebra as Balance`](courses/algebra-as-balance/README.md): three lessons for ages 12–15, grounded in openly accessible OER and focused on why equality-preserving moves work.
- [`Search by Halving`](courses/search-by-halving/README.md): three language-neutral lessons for ages 14+, grounded in official NIST and PSF references and focused on binary-search invariants rather than copied code.
- [`Probability as Evidence`](courses/probability-as-evidence/README.md): three lessons for ages 12+, grounded in OpenStax Introductory Statistics and focused on keeping denominators and reference groups visible.
- [`Functions as Change`](courses/functions-as-change/README.md): four lessons for ages 13+, grounded in OpenStax Precalculus and covering input-output rules, rate of change, bounded linear models, and a graph transformation lab where the learner manipulates vertex-form parameters against a target curve.
- [`Claims to Evidence`](courses/claims-to-evidence/README.md): three lessons for ages 12–16, grounded in the NIST/SEMATECH e-Handbook and focused on turning a broad claim into a falsifiable comparison.
- [`Samples to Conclusions`](courses/samples-to-conclusions/README.md): three lessons for ages 12–16, grounded in the NIST/SEMATECH e-Handbook and focused on sampling frames, variability, and bounded estimates.

## Creator intake boundary

Creator Studio accepts pasted content, a bounded set of authorized TXT/Markdown/selectable-text PDF files, or a sanitized HTTPS reference paired with transcript, excerpt, or notes. References may identify webpages, YouTube videos/playlists, and books, but Museion does not automatically fetch or transcribe them, bypass access controls, or retain a URL as a substitute for teachable source content. The normalized document hash binds the reference when one is present.
