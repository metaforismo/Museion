# Maia experience

Maia is contextual, secondary, and bounded. She receives the authoritative step snapshot and history, responds through a strict schema, and is checked for answer leaks, target validity, and stale state before any model text reaches the learner. One repair is allowed; otherwise the lesson uses verified deterministic guidance.

On desktop, Maia occupies a fixed right-hand reasoning rail below the lesson header. The lesson keeps the larger reading surface on the left; the rail uses a Codex-like transcript and bottom composer, a prominent mascot state, an explicit Grounded status, provider/model provenance, cancel and retry, input bounds, and duplicate-submit prevention. On smaller screens the same region returns to the document flow instead of covering content. The conversation follows only while the learner remains at the live edge; a “jump to latest” control appears after manual upward scrolling. The locked transfer removes Maia entirely.

Known limitation: authored lessons do not yet expose registered DOM target ids to live Maia; generated-runtime interventions do, and their annotation text is now rendered beside the registered target surface.
