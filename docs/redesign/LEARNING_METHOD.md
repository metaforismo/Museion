# The Museion method

Museion treats learning as a sequence of observable moves rather than content completion.

1. Attach claims to a trusted source.
2. Ask for a prediction or constructed move before explanation.
3. Check the response with deterministic rules.
4. Match only registered misconception patterns.
5. Offer bounded, fading guidance without revealing the answer.
6. Ask the learner to explain or apply the corrected rule.
7. Run one structurally related, unassisted immediate transfer check.
8. Record the observation with its limitations.

## Evidence language

Guided success informs support. Hint-free practice adds a different observation. Immediate near transfer shows performance on one related unassisted task. None proves long-term retention or broad mastery.

## Planned extensions

Delayed retrieval, interleaving, confidence calibration, worked-example fading, and a durable misconception notebook require new data and evaluation. They must not appear as active product features before those systems exist.
