# Learning science and claim boundaries

Museion applies established instructional mechanisms conservatively: retrieval before reveal, immediate feedback, worked reasoning, fading support, self-explanation prompts, misconception-specific remediation, and a separate immediate transfer task.

The implementation is testable, but the product has not established a general causal learning gain. Session scores are not durable mastery. A correct immediate near-transfer response is not far transfer. Retention requires delayed measurement.

Public materials should link directly to the research they cite and distinguish research motivation from Museion-specific measured evidence. Build Week evidence should report only observed runtime behavior, validator results, leak counts, and the immediate unassisted transfer outcome.

## Design hypotheses, not outcome claims

- Retrieval before reveal may create more useful effort than immediate explanation.
- Step-local feedback may be more actionable than end-of-problem grading.
- Fading hints may reduce dependence, but Museion has not yet measured whether its policy improves retention.
- Self-explanation and near transfer are observable learning moves; a single successful response is still only one observation.
