# Research and design sources

These sources motivate design choices; none establishes a Museion-specific learning effect.

## Learning and tutoring

- Bastani et al. (2025), randomized field experiment with nearly 1,000 Turkish high-school mathematics students: unrestricted GPT improved assisted practice but reduced subsequent unassisted performance; a guardrailed tutor improved practice while substantially mitigating that negative effect. The context, prompts, population, and outcome do not generalize automatically to Museion. [PNAS](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2422633122)
- Kestin et al. (2025), randomized crossover study with 194 students in one Harvard introductory physics course: an expert-designed AI tutor produced higher immediate post-test performance than an in-class active-learning lesson in that setting. This is not evidence for long-term retention or for Museion. [Scientific Reports](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-97652-6)
- VanLehn (2011), review comparing tutoring systems by interaction granularity. It supports step-based feedback as a design hypothesis, not a Museion effect size. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/00461520.2011.611369)
- Soderstrom and Bjork (2015), review distinguishing temporary performance during practice from durable learning. [Perspectives on Psychological Science](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1745691615569000)
- Tutor CoPilot (2024), randomized study of AI assistance for human tutors; useful evidence for bounded coaching, but not direct student-facing autonomous tutoring. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.03017)

## Child-centered design and curriculum

- UNICEF Guidance on AI and Children v3 frames safety, privacy, transparency, inclusion, fairness, and children’s best interests as product requirements. [UNICEF](https://www.unicef.org/innocenti/reports/policy-guidance-ai-children)
- UNESCO guidance recommends human-centered, privacy-preserving, age-appropriate validation of generative AI in education. [UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/guidance-generative-ai-education-and-research)
- Italy: MIM Indicazioni Nazionali 2025 materials are a future alignment source requiring status and subject-expert review before import. [MIM](https://www.mim.gov.it/)
- China: the Ministry of Education’s 2022 compulsory-education curriculum program and subject standards are a future alignment source; Museion has not produced an authoritative translation. [MOE announcement](https://www.moe.gov.cn/srcsite/A26/s8001/202204/t20220420_619921.html) · [English press release](https://en.moe.gov.cn/news/press_releases/202205/t20220507_625532.html)
