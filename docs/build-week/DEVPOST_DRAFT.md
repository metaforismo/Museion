## One-line description

Museion turns a trusted source into inspectable, deterministic learning interactions and records exactly what a learner applies without assistance.

## What it does

Museion normalizes a source into stable pages and hashes, grounds concepts and claims in exact spans, plans a typed course, and renders interactions whose truth is owned by deterministic reducers. Maia can coach only through a strict, leak-gated boundary. A final one-attempt near-transfer task disables Maia, hints, and solution reveal, then produces a reconciled evidence statement with explicit limitations.

## How we used Codex and GPT-5.6

Codex was the primary engineering agent and is also Museion's server-only local subscription runtime. The balanced policy uses GPT-5.6 Luna for source extraction, Terra for learning design, course generation, questions and Maia, and Sol for the publication critic and at most one typed repair. Exact schemas, source-span resolution, answer grading, leak detection, runtime transitions and publication remain deterministic. The judged golden path is checked in as a visibly labelled replay, so it remains reliable without credentials.

## Claim boundary

The demo records one immediate near-transfer observation. It does not prove durable mastery, far transfer, or learning efficacy. Codex plan usage consumes subscription quota even though it creates no separate API bill. Hosted deployment, video publication and final submission remain unverified external steps.
